Quinn — anticipating your next questions

"How much will this actually cost me?" Roughly $25,650 for the 75 credits at SNHU. If you're Pell-eligible (likely on an ED nurse salary with two dependents), expect ~$7,000 in grants. Many hospitals reimburse up to $5,250/year tax-free for employee education — worth checking your benefits. Net out-of-pocket likely lands around $13,000 over 22 months.

"Can I really do this around my shifts?" Yes — 8-week terms, async work, two courses at a time is standard for working students. We'll get you matched with an advisor who's worked with shift-based nurses.

"What does the next step look like?" Request transcripts from your community college (free if I send you the form), submit the application (no app fee for online programs), and we'll do a 20-minute call to confirm transfer credit and lock in the June 29 start.
