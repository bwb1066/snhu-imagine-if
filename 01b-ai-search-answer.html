Quinn — your SNHU advisor · cited in this answer

For a working nurse with an associate degree moving into healthcare administration, the practical answer comes down to three things: how many of your existing credits transfer, how fast the program lets you go around your shifts, and whether the school understands working-clinician realities. On those three filters, four online programs come up the most: SNHU's BS in Healthcare Administration, Arizona State University Online's BS Health Care Administration, Capella's BS Health Care Management, and WGU's BS Health and Human Services. Each has tradeoffs — SNHU usually wins on transfer-credit acceptance and tuition; ASU on brand recognition; Capella on FlexPath self-pacing; WGU on competency-based completion speed if you can study heavily. Quinn — the honest summary

Choose SNHU if you want the most transfer credit accepted, the lowest sticker price, structured 8-week terms with peer cohorts, and an advisor experienced with working clinicians. We're also a nonprofit, which means tuition reinvests into student support rather than shareholders.

Choose ASU Online if the brand-name recognition on a public R1 university matters to you, and you're willing to pay 65% more per credit for it.

Choose Capella if your schedule is irregular and you want pure self-pacing through FlexPath — best for people who can binge-study some weeks and disappear others.

Choose WGU if you have strong existing knowledge in the subject and want to test out of courses to finish faster — competency-based is great if you're disciplined and the material isn't new.
