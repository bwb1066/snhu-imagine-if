What students with your starting point did next

Based on outcomes of 12,400 SNHU BS-Healthcare-Administration graduates between 2019 and 2024 who entered with an associate-level clinical credential (RN, LPN, EMT, MA).

42% of grads

Moved into a leadership role within their existing employer

Charge nurse, unit manager, quality coordinator, case management lead. Median salary increase: $14,600 within 18 months of graduating.

31% of grads

Pursued a healthcare-focused MBA or MHA

Most continued at SNHU — admitted students with our BS receive a streamlined transfer. 4,100 grads have completed the bridge in the last 5 years.

19% of grads

Switched health systems for a step up

Took the bachelor's to a larger system in a director-track role. Median move was $22,800 in additional comp compared to staying put.

Quinn — what this means for you

The honest framing: this BS isn't a guarantee of any of these outcomes. It's a credential and a network. What turns it into the median-$98,200 charge-nurse role is the combination of degree + your existing ED experience + an internal conversation with your director once you're 6 months from graduating. I'll prompt you to start that conversation at the right time — usually around term 7. If a career switch to a new system is the goal instead, we'll start that earlier, around term 5.
