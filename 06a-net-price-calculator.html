Quinn — three honest things about this number

It assumes your Pell stays awarded. If your hospital gives you a raise during the program, Pell could shrink the next academic year. I'll re-run the math automatically when your FAFSA renews.

It assumes your hospital keeps its $5,250 benefit. Most do. If yours cuts it, you've still got the option to take a Direct Subsidized Loan (no interest while enrolled) for the gap — that's break-even given the salary lift after graduation.

It doesn't include books or fees. SNHU includes most textbooks in tuition — total fees over 22 months land around $250-$400 depending on capstone materials.
