Quinn — why I told you to decline the loans

Your tuition for the year is about $11,664 (34 credits × $342). Your grants + scholarships + employer reimbursement come to $14,595 — that's more than your tuition. The excess can go back to you as a refund check toward books, fees, or just bank for emergencies.

The $3,500 subsidized loan looks "free" because it's 0% while you're enrolled. But it's not free — it's borrowed. Federal student debt averages 6.53% after graduation, and the average grace-period-then-repayment timeline turns $3,500 into about $4,200 paid back. You don't need it. Decline it now and you can always re-request mid-year if circumstances change.
